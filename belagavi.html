Top Tourist Places in Bagalkot

Jamboti Hills

Jamboti Hills lies in the Western Ghats region near Belagavi. It is known for its cool climate and rich biodiversity. The region is suitable for trekking and camping activities. Dense forests and scenic views attract nature lovers. It provides a peaceful escape from urban life.

Kamal Basadi

Kamal Basadi is an ancient Jain temple located inside Belagavi Fort. It is famous for its lotus-shaped architectural design. The temple dates back to the 12th century. Intricate carvings reflect remarkable craftsmanship. It is an important spiritual and historical site.

Naviluteertha Dam

Naviluteertha Dam is built across the Malaprabha River. It offers scenic water views and peaceful surroundings. The dam area is popular for picnics and relaxation. Visitors enjoy the calm atmosphere and fresh air. It is a perfect family-friendly destination.

Rakaskop Reservoir

Rakaskop Reservoir is a serene water body near Belagavi. It supplies drinking water to the city. The location is known for its sunset and photography spots. Surrounded by hills and greenery, it looks picturesque. It is ideal for peaceful evening visits.

Parasgad Fort

Parasgad Fort is located near Savadatti town. The fort sits on a hilltop offering wide scenic views. It has historical significance connected to regional rulers. The trekking route is moderate and enjoyable. It is perfect for history lovers and adventurers.

Gokak Falls

Gokak Falls is one of the most spectacular waterfalls in Karnataka. The Ghataprabha River drops from a height of 52 meters creating a roaring cascade. It is often called the "Niagara of Karnataka" due to its wide horseshoe shape. The hanging bridge offers a breathtaking panoramic view. Best visited during monsoon season for full water flow.

Yellur Fort

Yellur Fort is a beautiful hill fort located near Belagavi city. It is a popular trekking destination among adventure enthusiasts. The fort offers stunning sunrise and sunset views. The peaceful surroundings make it ideal for weekend trips. It combines history and natural beauty.

Godachinmalki Falls

Godachinmalki Falls is a hidden natural gem surrounded by lush greenery. It is perfect for nature lovers and photography enthusiasts. The waterfall becomes more powerful during monsoon season. Visitors can enjoy a calm and less crowded environment. It is ideal for a refreshing day trip.

Military Mahadeva Temple

Military Mahadeva Temple is a famous Shiva temple in Belagavi. It attracts thousands of devotees every year. The temple architecture is simple yet spiritually powerful. It is especially crowded during Mahashivaratri festival. A peaceful place for prayer and meditation.

Hidkal Dam

Hidkal Dam is one of the largest reservoirs in Karnataka. It is built across the Ghataprabha River. The scenic drive to the dam offers beautiful countryside views. Visitors enjoy boating and picnic activities nearby. It is a great place for nature and water lovers.

Belagavi Fort

Built in the 13th century, Belagavi Fort is a historic landmark of the city. It reflects Indo-Islamic architecture and strong medieval defense design. The famous Kamal Basadi Jain temple is located inside the fort complex. The fort walls and gateways showcase impressive craftsmanship. It is a must-visit destination for history enthusiasts.
